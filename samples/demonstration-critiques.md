## Marlowe & Co. (landlord pitch)

**On positioning:**
> "Marlowe & Co. is a warm, welcoming café bringing quality coffee, fresh food and genuine community connection to the heart of Yass. We want to create a space where locals feel at home from their very first visit — a true community hub for Comur Street."

This line could be pasted into any pitch for any café on Comur Street — nothing in it is actually about Comur Street. What does a regular walk past to get to Marlowe & Co. specifically? Name the actual thing, not the feeling.

**On the financial projection:**
> "We project average daily trade of 55 covers at an average transaction value of $13... Food cost is budgeted at the industry-standard 30%, and we're targeting break-even within six months."

There's no reasoning shown behind any of these figures — no comparable trading data, no foot-traffic count, no supplier quote behind the food cost line. A number with nothing behind it reads as asserted, not estimated. Where does the 55 actually come from?

**On operator credibility:**
> "I have always been passionate about coffee and hospitality, and have dreamed of running my own café for years. I've spent the last two years researching the industry and visiting cafés around the country."

A landlord isn't asking whether you love coffee — they're asking whether you can hold a P&L together through a slow month, and passion doesn't answer that. What's actually in your background that proves you can run the operational side under pressure?

## Southern Roast Co. (investor pitch)

**On positioning:**
> "Southern Roast Co. is building a much-loved specialty coffee brand in the Southern Tablelands, known for exceptional coffee, warm hospitality and a strong connection to our local community."

Every claim in this line — "much-loved," "exceptional," "strong connection" — could describe any specialty coffee pitch. What tells an investor Southern Roast Co. specifically is worth 15% of the business?

**On the financial projection:**
> "Our existing café currently generates strong, consistent trade. We project the second site will perform similarly, reaching profitability within its first year. Combined, we forecast... approximately $850,000 in annual revenue, with a target gross margin of 35%."

"Generates strong, consistent trade" describes a number without giving one, and "will perform similarly" assumes the second site transfers without saying why. What's the actual current revenue figure for site one?
